The Economic Contributions of Former Colonies to Britain's Economy

As Malayan tin production increased, British GDP rose, demonstrating how colonial resources contributed to Britain's industrial and economic growth during this era.

Sources:

1. Feinstein, C. (1972). National income, output and expenditure of the United Kingdom 1855-1965 . Cambridge University Press.

2. Kees, K. G., & Fink-Jensen, J. (2023). Tin Production. IISH Data Collection. hdl:10622/WOTROV

Impact of Colonial Policies on Modern Inequality in Former Colonies

Colonized countries generally show higher Gini coefficients, indicating greater income inequality compared to non-colonized countries, reflecting lasting economic disparities.
